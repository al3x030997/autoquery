MSWL SAMPLE — DETAILED TEXT ANALYSIS (48 profiles)
====================================================

First-pass deep-dive of 48 ManuscriptWishlist agent profiles captured through
L0→L1 (Note-Taker prompt v2.0, Sonnet 4.5). Internal validation material —
nothing here has touched the product database.


HEADLINE FINDINGS
-----------------

- Capture worked. 48/48 pages fetched, 0 parse failures on the LLM side.
  Median 3.5 preference sections per profile, max 15. Total run ~83 minutes,
  API cost ~$2.
- Canon coverage is well below v1 targets on every facet. Subject 51.5%,
  audience 53.1%, hard_no 0.4%. Targets are 90% / 90% / 70%.
- Hard-no is effectively unmapped (2 of 463 terms matched). This is the
  single biggest finding of the run — a vocabulary mismatch that needs
  redesign, not patches.
- Identity extraction is noisier than expected. Only 83% of profiles
  yielded a name. The same 8 profiles fail name + organization + role +
  availability together → one specific template variant the parser misses.
- Prompt is self-aware. Mean 3.1 MISSING flags vs 1.4 INFERRED per profile
  — the Note-Taker prefers to admit absence over hallucinating. Good
  signal for downstream trust.


1 · CANON COVERAGE (see coverage.png)
-------------------------------------

  Facet    | Mapped | Total | Coverage | v1 target
  ---------+--------+-------+----------+----------
  subject  |   308  |  598  |  51.5%   |   90%
  audience |   112  |  211  |  53.1%   |   90%
  hard_no  |     2  |  463  |   0.4%   |   70%

Subject at ~52%: every second genre label agents write has no home in the
current canon. Most of the gap is synonyms ("feminist horror", "upmarket
commercial fiction") that need aliasing, not new concepts. A small number
are genuine category gaps — mostly horror subgenres.

Audience at ~53%: the misses are largely normalization artefacts. The
Note-Taker emits `young_adult` and `middle_grade` (underscore form) but the
alias map keys them with spaces. A one-line normalizer fix would lift this
facet to ~80% without adding any aliases.

Hard-no at 0.4% is the alarm bell. `hard_nos.yaml` was designed before the
Note-Taker integration; the phrases agents actually write ("graphic sexual
violence", "gratuitous gore", "torture porn", "sexual assault on-page")
bear almost no resemblance to the compact tags in that file. This facet
needs a rewrite, not patches.


2 · PROFILE STRUCTURE (see section_histogram.png, section_types.png)
--------------------------------------------------------------------

- Median 3.5 sections per agent, max 15.
- 12 profiles returned zero preference sections. Two failure modes:
  - Group A (8 profiles): no name, no sections → different LLM template
    variant the prompt doesn't handle.
  - Group B (4 profiles: brandy-vallance, elizabeth-poteet, emelie-burl,
    mark-falkin): sections use prose headings like "### GENERAL (All Ages
    — Eclectic)" instead of the expected "### [SECTION LABEL]" bracket
    form. Parser needs a no-brackets fallback.
- 94% of sections are hybrid (audience + genre). Agents almost never
  split preferences on a single axis. The matcher should weight the
  combination of axes, not each axis independently.


3 · IDENTITY COMPLETENESS (see identity_fill.png)
-------------------------------------------------

- Name, organization, role, availability all cluster at ~83%. The same
  8 profiles fail all four together — strong signal of a single template
  variant rather than independent extraction failures.
- Email only in 52% of profiles. Many agents route through a portal
  (QueryManager, Submittable) rather than listing an address. The
  `submission_portal` field at 81% corroborates this — it's working
  as designed.
- Pronouns at 12%. Reflects real MSWL profile sparsity, not a parser bug.


4 · HARD-NOS (see hardno_buckets.png)
-------------------------------------

- All four buckets (content / format / trope / category) fire in 29–52%
  of profiles — agents do declare exclusions.
- The `trope` bucket lags at 29% because many agents fold trope dislikes
  into free-text `content` prose rather than listing them separately.
  The prompt could disambiguate more aggressively.
- Combined with the 0.4% canon-mapping rate: we have the data, we just
  can't filter on it yet.


5 · CONFIDENCE FLAGS (see confidence.png)
-----------------------------------------

- INFERRED mean 1.4, NUANCED 1.1, MISSING 3.1 per profile.
- MISSING > INFERRED is the healthy pattern. The prompt is more willing
  to admit absence than to guess. That's exactly the behavior we want
  feeding L2/L3 — it stops downstream layers from building on fabricated
  signal.


6 · COMP TITLES (see comps.png)
-------------------------------

- 33/48 profiles list at least one comp. Median 7, max 110.
- The outlier at 110 is one agent who lists favorite books broadly, not
  per-section comps. The matcher should cap per-profile contribution to
  prevent that profile from dominating similarity scores.


7 · UNMAPPED VOCABULARY LEADERBOARDS
------------------------------------

See unmapped_subject.png, unmapped_audience.png, unmapped_hard_no.png.

Subject: Green bars (≥5 profiles) are LOCAL-extension candidates. At
48 profiles the bar is high — expect many more to clear it as the
sample grows toward the Step 4 target of 200. Obvious LOCAL candidates
already: gothic horror, psychological horror.

Audience: Small absolute counts but 4 of 6 are LOCAL candidates. Most
are normalization issues ("adults" vs adult, "teens" vs young_adult,
underscore-form vs space-form).

Hard-no: this is the facet that needs most attention. The unmapped
terms are long, specific phrases written in natural language —
nothing in the current alias file comes close.


8 · IMMEDIATE ACTION LIST
-------------------------

1. Audit `canon/hard_nos.yaml` against the top-50 unmapped hard_no
   terms. It's not an alias problem; it's a vocabulary mismatch that
   needs redesign.

2. Fix the normalizer: map underscore-form audience tokens
   (young_adult, middle_grade, new_adult, picture_books) to the
   space-form keys. One-line change, lifts audience coverage to ~80%.

3. Add aliases for obvious token-overlap candidates in subject. Low
   risk, immediate coverage lift. Estimate: +15–20 percentage points
   with a 60-line edit.

4. Add LOCAL:gothic_horror and LOCAL:psychological_horror — both
   cleared the 5-profile bar already.

5. Investigate the 12 zero-section profiles. Group A (8) and Group B
   (4) have different root causes; Group B is fixable with a parser
   regex relaxation.

6. Rerun `scripts/canon_dryrun.py` after aliases/extensions land. No
   re-crawl needed — it reads cached notes_parsed/.

7. Do not treat this as v1 readiness. 48 profiles is a diagnostic,
   not a lock. Step 4's 200-profile production run is still required
   per docs/features/16_l2_canonicalization.md.
